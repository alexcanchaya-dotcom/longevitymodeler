Nutrition & Longevity

ü•ó Key Finding

Mediterranean diet adds 10+ years to lifespan. Multiple studies show optimal nutrition can add 5-13 years vs poor diet.

The Power of Diet

Your diet impacts every aspect of aging - from cellular function to disease risk. Research shows nutrition accounts for 30-40% of longevity variance.

Why Nutrition Matters

  • Inflammation: Diet is #1 driver of chronic inflammation
  • Oxidative Stress: Antioxidants protect cells from damage
  • Metabolic Health: Controls insulin, blood sugar, cholesterol
  • Gut Microbiome: Feeds beneficial bacteria
  • Gene Expression: Nutrients activate longevity genes

Mediterranean Diet: Gold Standard

Impact: +10-13 years lifespan

Core Components

  • Olive oil as primary fat (4+ tbsp/day)
  • Vegetables: 5-9 servings daily
  • Fruits: 2-4 servings daily
  • Legumes: 3+ servings/week
  • Fish: 2-3 servings/week
  • Nuts: 1 oz (30g) daily
  • Whole grains vs refined
  • Moderate red wine (optional)
  • Limited red meat, dairy, sweets

PREDIMED Study (7,500 participants)

30% reduction in cardiovascular events
Equivalent to adding 10+ years healthy life

Foods That Add Years

Vegetables & Fruits

Impact: Each daily serving = -5% mortality

  • Leafy greens: kale, spinach, arugula
  • Cruciferous: broccoli, cauliflower, Brussels sprouts
  • Berries: blueberries, strawberries (high antioxidants)
  • Citrus: vitamin C, flavonoids
  • Tomatoes: lycopene for heart health

Healthy Fats

  • Olive Oil: Monounsaturated fats, polyphenols
  • Fatty Fish: Omega-3s reduce inflammation (salmon, sardines, mackerel)
  • Nuts: Almonds, walnuts - 28% lower mortality with daily consumption
  • Avocados: Heart-healthy fats, fiber, potassium

Protein Sources

  • Fish: Best protein for longevity
  • Legumes: Beans, lentils - fiber + protein
  • Moderate Poultry: Better than red meat
  • Eggs: Complete protein, moderate consumption
  • Plant Proteins: Tofu, tempeh

Foods That Subtract Years

Impact: -14 years healthspan with high consumption

  • Added sugars, artificial ingredients
  • Trans fats, excessive sodium
  • Packaged snacks, sodas
  • Fast food, processed meats

Foods to Minimize

  • Red Meat: >100g/day increases mortality 20%
  • Processed Meat: 50g/day increases risk 18%
  • Sugary Drinks: Each daily serving = +7% mortality
  • Refined Carbs: White bread, pasta, pastries
  • Excessive Alcohol: >2 drinks/day harmful

Eating Patterns That Work

Time-Restricted Eating

Eating within 8-12 hour window:

  • Enhances autophagy (cellular cleanup)
  • Improves insulin sensitivity
  • May extend lifespan 10-20% (animal studies)
  • Example: 8am-6pm or 10am-8pm eating window

Caloric Restriction (with adequate nutrition)

  • Moderate CR: 10-20% below needs shows benefits
  • Longevity Genes: Activates SIRT1, AMPK pathways
  • Human Studies: Improves all biomarkers
  • Caution: Must maintain adequate nutrition

Practical Guidelines

üéØ Daily Longevity Diet Template

Breakfast: Oats + berries + nuts
Lunch: Large salad + olive oil + fish/legumes
Dinner: Vegetables + whole grain + lean protein
Snacks: Fruit, nuts, vegetables
Drinks: Water, green tea, coffee

Key Principles

  • Eat mostly plants (70-80% of calories)
  • Choose whole over processed
  • Healthy fats over saturated fats
  • Moderate portions, stop at 80% full
  • Variety of colors on plate
  • Cook at home mostly
  • Mindful eating, no distractions

Supplements Worth Considering

  • Vitamin D: If deficient (most people are)
  • Omega-3 (EPA/DHA): If not eating fish 2-3x/week
  • Magnesium: Common deficiency
  • B12: Especially for vegetarians, older adults

Note: Food first, supplements as backup. Consult healthcare provider.

Getting Started

  1. Add 1-2 more vegetable servings daily
  2. Replace refined grains with whole grains
  3. Swap butter/margarine for olive oil
  4. Add 1 serving fish per week
  5. Reduce processed food by 50%
  6. Gradually build toward Mediterranean pattern
